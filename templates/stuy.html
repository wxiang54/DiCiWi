I wanted to say much more than I could fit in the 156 characters allotted in the yearbook, so this'll have to do: I arrived to Stuyveant High School as a turtle. This was not just because I was really slow to catch onto stuff (or because I fw Netlogo), but also because my transition to high school had retreated me so far into my shell that my field of vision had been reduced to a single speck of light. At a school like Stuy, however, I find that worldview and mindset hard to sustain for long. Enter amazingly supportive friends and staff (highkey shoutouts to the CS dept), and although I can't wholeheartedly say that I'm not leaving Stuy with any of the regrets I've accrued throughout my four years, what I'm definitely leaving with is four years of memories I wouldn't have had laid out any other way, and which I can forever look back on with tidings of nostalgia. Now I'm a koala. I'm still painfully slow, but I've been exposed to the wilderness of life. I've also forgotten how to swim. Thank you, Stuyvesant. - WX
